Section 05
Motion Graphics Frames
Hard Rule — Full Frame
Every motion graphic must render on its own dedicated full-screen frame. Motion graphics never appear on top of A-roll video footage. Each frame is a complete visual unit with its own branded background in the pillar accent color.
Frame anatomy
Logo zone
Headline
Body copy
PILLAR TAG
1
Full-screen background
The entire frame fills with the pillar's accent color. No video playing behind it. No transparency. Solid fill from edge to edge.
2
Logo always present
MyoSynex logo lockup appears in the upper zone of every motion graphic frame. Use the white reverse version on colored backgrounds. Logo must clear its required safe space (see packaging guide).
3
Barlow Condensed — all type
All text on motion graphic frames uses Barlow Condensed. Headline: Bold 800. Body copy: SemiBold 600. Pillar tag: Bold 700, all caps, tracked wide.
4
Slide-in animation
Frame slides in from the right. Duration: 0.3s. Ease: cubic ease-out. Holds for content duration. Exits with a cut or quick slide-left.
5
No mixed pillars
A motion graphic frame uses exactly one pillar color. Never mix colors from different pillars within a single video. All MG frames in one video share the same pillar background.

Section 06
Standard Video Structure
#SegmentDurationContentNotes
1Intro3.5 secBranded intro animationNavy bg · dot animation · logo · pillar name · chime
2Hook0–5 secA-roll — speaker's opening statementCaptions active. No B-roll over the hook — let the face hold attention.
3BodyVariableA-roll with B-roll cutawaysCaptions always on. Motion graphic frames placed at key points (see rule below).
4Motion Graphic Frames2.5–4 sec eachKey stats, terms, stepsUse at key moments — wherever the content benefits from being read, not just heard. Every MG is a dedicated full-screen frame.
5Outro3 secBranded outro animationSame pillar color as intro · full logo · chime (softer) · fade to black
When to Use a Motion Graphic Frame
Use a motion graphic frame whenever the speaker references a stat, a list, a term, or a step-by-step instruction that benefits from being read, not just heard. Place them at natural pauses — never cut to an MG in the middle of a sentence.
Section 07
Brand Voice — Editing Considerations

The Proactive Ager audience is 50+, intelligent, and does not respond to hype. Every editing decision should reinforce the brand promise: "Strong aging starts within."

DoDo Not
Keep pacing measured — this is not a hype reelUse rapid-fire jump cuts or flash transitions
Let the speaker finish thoughts before cuttingCut mid-word or mid-breath for effect
Use B-roll that shows real functional movement (walking, lifting, cooking)Use youth-focused or gym-bodybuilder B-roll
Keep music subtle — it supports the voice, not competes with itUse loud or trendy audio that overshadows the content
Color-grade to a clean, slightly warm, clinical-modern feelAdd heavy filters, oversaturated looks, or moody dark grades
Use motion graphics to reinforce key points — crediblyOverload the video with text overlays, flying graphics, or decorative animations
Maintain pillar color consistency throughout the full videoMix pillar accent colors within a single video
Ensure captions are perfectly synced to speechAllow caption drift — unsynchronized captions break trust immediately
